Laguna's Economic Status

Economic Status

Laguna has a vibrant economy — producing coconuts, rice, sugar, citrus fruits, lanzones, and other goods worth millions of pesos. Tourist attractions like Pagsanjan Falls, Calamba and Los Baños hot springs, Mount Makiling, Caliraya Lake, and many more draw visitors. The areas near Metro Manila like Las Pinas have grown industrialized, whereas the inner cities continue to engage in agricultural production or pursue cottage and small-scale enterprises. Because of the presence of large automobile manufacturers in the City of Santa Rosa Laguna is known as the "Detroit of the Philippines." It is home to a thriving automotive sector, with plants belonging to Toyota Motor Philippines, Nissan Motor Philippines, Honda Philippines Incorporated, and Mitsubishi Motors Philippines. It is also known as the Philippines' Silicon Valley due to the sheer large number of electronic and semiconductor industries operating in the province. Furthermore, Laguna is also known as the Resort Capital of the Philippines, with over 700 hot spring resorts located mostly in Calamba and Los Baños.

Laguna's Inflation rate also increased by 4.8 percent in September 2022 from 3.7 percent in August 2022. Moreover, this is equal to the recorded inflation rate in the same month of 2021 at 4.8 percent. The average inflation rate for the first nine months of the year stood at 3.6 percent.

A Graph showing Laguna's Inflation Rate.

The economy of the province of Laguna was valued at PhP 922.3 billion in 2021, the highest among the five provinces and one highly urbanized city (HUC) in the region. As measured by the Gross Provincial Domestic Product (GPDP), the economy of the province posted a growth of 3.9 percent in 2021, a rebound from a -6.0 percent decline in the previous year.
